The primary revenue engine — and the #1 reason members join

Insurance Is the Engine. Everything Else Is the Platform.

For many members, insurance is the reason they join — and it's the reason they stay. A small business owner in aesthetic medicine is a tiny, high-risk buyer to any carrier. They pay retail rates, have no leverage, and often can't access affordable coverage at all. When AFMA pools thousands of these businesses together, the association becomes a large group buyer. Carriers compete. Rates drop significantly. That savings gap — between what they pay today and what they pay as an AFMA member — is the core value proposition.

⚡

The Critical Principle — All Insurance Revenue Flows Back to the Association

Every insurance enrollment that happens through AFMA generates a commission that flows directly back into the association. This is not a side arrangement — it is the primary revenue mechanism. Members pay retail today. As AFMA members, they access group rates. AFMA earns a commission from the carrier on each enrollment. The member saves money. The association builds revenue. Both win.

This is what makes insurance the engine rather than just a benefit. It doesn't just attract members — it funds the entire operation as membership grows.

Olivarch — Purpose-Built Coverage for Aesthetic Medicine

Olivarch is an insurance product built specifically for the risk profile of aesthetic medicine — something generic carriers don't fully understand or price correctly. AFMA is the distribution channel that puts Olivarch in front of thousands of clinics who already trust the association.

Membership in AFMA and enrollment in Olivarch are separate decisions — neither requires the other. A member can join AFMA without Olivarch. A practitioner may access Olivarch independently. But for members who do enroll, AFMA earns a commission, and the member gets purpose-built coverage they can't find anywhere else.

AFMA creates the relationship and the trust. Olivarch creates the product. This is the connection between the two entities.

Questions that need answers to move forward

💰 Financial Model

?Year 1 Member Goal: What is the realistic target — and what is the aspirational target? These need to be two separate numbers going into the financial model.
?Year 2 Member Goal: What growth rate is the model built on — and what marketing spend is required to hit it?
?Benefit Budget per Member: How much is AFMA allocating per member per year (or per month) to fund the member benefit stack? This number drives everything — what benefits are possible, what tier they land in, and what the net margin looks like.

🛡️ Insurance & Revenue

?Commission Structure: What is the confirmed commission rate AFMA earns per insurance enrollment across each product line (malpractice, general liability, CapCare, workers comp, Olivarch)?
?Olivarch Enrollment Model: Will Olivarch be available exclusively to AFMA members, or available to non-members as well? This affects both positioning and revenue projection.
?Carrier Relationships: Which carriers are confirmed vs. in negotiation? What is the timeline to having group rates available at launch?

⚖️ Regulatory & Lobbying

?Gov. Relations Resource: Who is AFMA engaging to lead the lobbying and regulatory arm — internal hire, outside firm, or existing partner?
